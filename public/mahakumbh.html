Maha Kumbh Mela

The World's Largest Spiritual Gathering

Sacred Journey to Enlightenment

Maha Kumbh is the world's largest spiritual gathering, held once every 12 years at the sacred confluence of the Ganges, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati rivers. Millions of devotees, saints, and seekers come together for this divine event where a single holy dip is believed to cleanse one's sins and lead to salvation. Experience the vibrant processions of Naga Sadhus, enlightening spiritual discourses, devotional music, and the profound energy of collective consciousness.

Why Attend Maha Kumbh?

Witness ancient traditions come alive, participate in sacred rituals, and immerse yourself in India's spiritual heritage. The Maha Kumbh offers a unique opportunity for personal transformation amidst the largest peaceful gathering on Earth.

Journey Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Prayagraj

Arrive in the holy city, check into your accommodation, and visit Triveni Sangam (confluence point) for evening aarti ceremony.

Day 2: Sacred Bath & Exploration

Take the auspicious morning dip at Sangam, visit Akshayavat (immortal banyan tree), and explore the Kumbh Mela grounds.

Day 3: Spiritual Enlightenment

Attend discourses by revered saints, experience cultural performances, and participate in evening Ganga aarti.
